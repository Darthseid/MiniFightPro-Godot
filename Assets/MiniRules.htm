MINIATURE FIGHT RULES AND HELP

Unit Creation

Unit Creation is the customization place for Miniature Fight. This is where you create and customize Units, Miniatures, Weapons, and their abilities. There are already multiple presets available that you can edit.

Miniature Fight uses six-sided dice to resolve events. If a value or effect says D6, it means a random number between 1 & 6 (inclusive) is generated. If a value or effect says D3, it means a random number between 1 & 3 (inclusive) is generated.

Each unit has a name, Type, 🏃, 🪨, ❤️, 🏳️, 🛡️,
➗, & 🔮. 

·         🏃 is the Move Variable. It represents how far the unit can normally move. Format is (0.0)

·         🪨 is the Hardness variable. It represents how tough the unit is. This is generally a number between 1-12.

·         🏳️ is the Bravery Variable. This represents the ability to pass Bravery Tests or Shellshock tests. This is generally a number between 5-9.

·         🛡️ is the Defense Variable. It represents the ability to block injury hits. It is always a number between 2-7.

·         🔮 is the Dodge Variable. It represents the ability to dodge any injury hit. It is always a number between 2-7.

·         ➗ is Damage Resistance. It works on all forms of damage. It is always a number between 2-7.

Each unit has multiple abilities that you can give it. And of course, a unit would be nothing without their Miniatures. Each Miniature in Miniature Fight has a name, ❤️, LowHealth, and weapons. ❤️ is Health. When a mini’s ❤️ runs out, they are dead. Format is (0). LowHealth is a threshold for generally larger miniatures. When a mini’s ❤️ is less than or equal to LowHealth, they have a -1 Hit Modifier. Weapons are what the miniatures use to fight.

Unit Types determine the size and purpose of a unit. The valid Unit Types are Infantry, Character, Mounted, Monster, Building, & Vehicle.

Each Weapon in Miniature Fight has a Name, 🏹, 🤺, 🔫, 💪, 🕳️, & 🩸.

·         🏹 is the attack range of the weapon. Weapons with an attack range less than 1.0 are considered melee weapons. The format is (0.0).

·         🤺 is the number of attacks a weapon has. Format is (0). 🤺 can also be D3, D6, or a multiplier of D3 or D6 with integer addition. (e.g. 2D3+4).

·         🔫 is the Accuracy of a weapon. This matters for the Hit Sequence. This is always a number between 2-6.

·         💪 is the Strength of a weapon. This is generally a number between 1-18.

·         🕳️ is the armor penetration of a weapon. This is always a number between 0 & -5.

·         🩸 is the Damage a weapon deals. Format is (0). 🩸 can also be D3, D6, or a multiplier of D3 or D6 with integer addition. (e.g. 2D3+4).

Weapons in Miniature Fight can also have multiple abilities.

Once you have created your Units, Miniatures, and Weapons, make sure to save them so you can use them in the game!

Start Game

Press Start Game, select a unit for the first team, and then select a unit for the 2nd team. Press Play and both units will be loaded into the game.

 

At the beginning of the match, pre-game checks and decisions will be made. You can adjust the starting distance of both units to your liking. When you’re finished, you can press Begin game. Once pressed, Miniature Fight will randomly pick a starting unit and the game will begin!

 

The Active Player is whomever turn it is. The Inactive Player is the other player.

 

The Stages in Miniature Fight are Starting Stage, Move Stage, Shoot Stage, Charge Stage, Fight Stage, and End Stage.

·        Starting Stage is when the beginning of turn/round checks are made. This is also when the Shellshock test is taken. If a unit is missing over half of the minis it started with, or it is a single-model unit that is missing over half of its health, it is considered Understrength. Understrength units take a Shellshock Test at the beginning of its Starting Stage. If the Shellshock Test fails, it is considered Shell-shocked. Shell-shocked units risk losing Minis whenever they Retreat.

o   For the Shellshock Test, two dice (D6) are rolled. If the sum of the two dice is equal to or above the 🏳️ of the unit being tested (after modifiers), the test is passed. Otherwise, it will fail.

·        Movement Stage is when units move. Units can elect to either make a Standard Move, or Rush.

o   During a Standard Move, a unit moves equal to its 🏃 plus modifiers.

o   During a Rush, a unit moves equal to its 🏃 and D6 inches plus modifiers. Units in Fight Range cannot Rush. If a unit rushes, they generally cannot charge or shoot.

o   Units that try to move while they’re in Fight Range (within 1 inch of an enemy unit) are Falling Back. Units that Fall Back normally cannot shoot or charge.

o   Aircraft have different rules for movement. They can move up to 100 inches in any direction, but they must move a minimum of 20 inches if able.

·        Shoot Stage is when units shoot. Each model shoots at the enemy unit. Each model shoots all weapons they have that are within range of the enemy unit.

o   The Check Valid Shooting Step of the Shooting Phase determines whether a weapon is eligible to shoot at the enemy unit. Units except for Monsters and Vehicles cannot fire non-pistol weapons while it is within Fight Range of an enemy unit. Blast weapons can never be fired within Fight Range.

o   The Modifier step introduces additive modifiers, subtractive modifiers, and rerolls to weapons that shoot. The Hit Modifier has a minimum limit of -1 and a maximum limit of +1. The Injury Modifier has a minimum limit of -1 and a maximum limit of +1. The 🛡️has a maximum limit of +1. If a Monster or Vehicle fires a weapon in Fight Range, there is a -1 to Hit Modifier.

o    The Hit sequence determines based on the number of shots; how many attacks hit the target. For each attack D6 is rolled and modifiers are included. For each die roll greater than or equal to the 🔫, a hit is scored against the target. Failed dice may be rerolled based on the weapon/unit ability.

o   The Injury sequence determines based on the number of hits; how many hits count as injury hits. For each hit, D6 is rolled, and modifiers are included. For each die roll greater than or equal to the Injury threshold, a injury hit is scored against the target. Failed dice may be rerolled based on the weapon/unit ability. ·        Charge Stage is when units within 12” of each other can charge into Fight Range (1”). A Charge Test is equal to 2D6 + Modifiers. If it is passed, the charging unit is moved next to the target unit and fights first.

·        Fight Stage only occurs if both units are within Fight Range of each other. If so, Fight Order is determined as follows:

o   The player who fights first uses all their melee weapons on their opponent. If their opponent survives, they then use all their melee weapons on the player who fought first.

o   The Hit Sequence, Injury Sequence, Defense Sequence, and Damage Sequence used during the Fight Stage are identical to the one used for the Shoot Stage, but different modifiers can be in play.

o   If a model has multiple melee weapons, it uses them all.

Some abilities deal Pure Damage. Pure Damage bypasses the attack sequence and immediately damages the affected units. Unlike regular damage, Excess pure damage is dealt to other models in the unit.

To win the game, a unit must destroy all miniatures in the enemy unit. If both units are dead, it is considered a draw.

Remember the golden rule of Games. If an ability contradicts the game rules, the ability takes precedence.

 

 

Unit Abilities

There are multiple abilities units can have in Miniature Fight. These abilities can enhance a unit and make them harder to defeat.

 

The current list is as follows:

  • Aircraft: This unit is considered an aircraft and has Aircraft Movement rules.
  • Alien Terror: At the beginning of a round, you can force the enemy to take a Shellshock Test. Then this ability is removed.
  • Charge After Rush: This unit can charge even if it rushed this turn.
  • Charge Bonus: This unit receives a +X modifier to its Charge rolls.
  • Close to Shoot: Enemy units further than 12 inches away from this unit cannot shoot at it.
  • Demonic Grief: This unit has a +1 modifier to the Shellshock Test. Whenever this unit passes a Shellshock Test, Reanimate is triggered. Enemy units have a -1 Modifier to the Shellshock Test. Whenever an enemy unit becomes Shellshocked, they suffer D3 Pure Damage.
  • Fight After Melee Death: When a model in this unit dies, it can still fight an enemy unit that it is within Fight Range of this turn.
  • Fight Stances: This unit can choose different Fighting Stances at the beginning of the Fight Stage. You can choose to give all melee weapons this stage Bonus Hits, all melee weapons Hard Hits, or to have a -1 to Hit Modifier applied for attacks against this unit. The effect resets when the Fight Stage ends.
  • First Strike: This unit generally fights first in a Fight Stage.
  • Flying: This unit is considered a flying unit for rules purposes.
  • Grim: Enemy units within 12” of this unit have a -1 modifier in the Shellshock Test. After Round 2, this unit has a +1 Injury Modifier when shooting at Shellshocked enemies. Shellshocked enemies have a -1 Hit Modifier towards shooting this unit.
  • Guaranteed Rush: This unit always advances 6 inches when advancing.
  • Hive Mind: This unit has a D6 Modifier for Shellshock Tests.
  • Minus Hit: causes all attacks against this unit to have a -1 to Hit Modifier.
  • Minus Hit Melee: causes all ranged attacks against this unit to have a -1 to Hit Modifier.
  • Minus Hit Ranged: causes all ranged attacks against this unit to have a -1 to Hit Modifier.
  • Move After Shooting: This unit can move after its Shoot Stage.
  • Move Back: Whenever an enemy moves within 9 inches of this unit, this unit can then move up to D6 inches in any direction.

Weapon Abilities

 

There are multiple abilities in Miniature Fight that make weapons more powerful. Here is the list:

 

·         Anti-Type: This weapon has a greater chance of dealing critical Injuries against enemies of a certain type. A roll of X or more counts as a critical Injury.

·         Assault: This weapon can be used after Rushing.

·         Blast: This weapon deals an additional attack roll for every 5 models in the unit. Blast weapons can’t be used in Fight Range.

·         Bonus Hits (x): Each critical hit by this weapon deals an additional X bonus hits. Critical Hits are unmodified die rolls of 6 by default.

·         Conversion: When shooting at enemy units more than 12 inches away, this weapon has Critical Hits on 4, 5, and 6. Critical Hits automatically hit.

·         Hard Hits: Critical Hits by this weapon automatically penetrate the target unit.

·         Heavy: If the unit didn’t move this turn, this weapon has a +1 to Hit Modifier in the next Shoot Stage.

·         Ignores Cover: Units do not benefit from cover when they are shot at by this weapon. Currently, there is no cover or terrain in Miniature Fight, but certain abilities give units a +1 🛡️ boost for being in cover.

·         Obliterating: Critical Injuries by this weapon cannot be saved against and automatically deal damage. Critical Injuries are unmodified die rolls of 6 by default.

·         One Hit Reroll: Up to one die generated by this weapon in the Hit Sequence is rerolled if it fails.

·         One Injury Reroll: Up to one die generated by this weapon in the Injury Sequence is rerolled if it fails.

·         Perilous: After shooting with this weapon, D6 is rolled. On a 1, the model who shot the weapon is slain. If the model who shot it was an Infantry or Vehicle Unit, they suffer 3 Pure Damage instead.

·         Pike: Whenever this unit charges, its melee weapons have a +1 to Injury Modifier this turn.

·         Pistol: This weapon can be fired even within Fight Range. If this weapon is used, non-Pistol weapons cannot be used in this phase.

·         Rapid (X): When shooting at a target within half-range, this weapon makes X additional attacks.

·         Reroll Hit: Whenever this weapon attacks, reroll all failed hit rolls.

·         Reroll Hit Ones: Whenever this weapon attacks, reroll all unmodified hit rolls of 1.

·         Reroll Injury: Whenever this weapon attacks, all failed Injury rolls.

·         Reroll Injury Ones: Whenever this weapon attacks, reroll all unmodified Injury rolls of 1.
